Hamburger

Description

A hamburger is a sandwich that contains minced or plant-based meat, agglutinated in the form of a steak cooked on the grill or grill, although it can also be fried or baked. Outside the Spanish-speaking world, it is more common to find the American name burger , shortening of hamburger. It comes in a light bread roll divided in two that has an oval or rounded shape.

Ingredients

  • Burger buns
  • 1 and 1/2 pounds lean ground beef
  • 1/2 onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup Cheddar cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1 ounce envelope dry onion soup mix
  • 1 clove garlic minced
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat an outdoor grill for high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  2. Meanwhile, combine ground beef, onion, cheese, egg, onion soup mix, minced garlic, garlic powder, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, parsley, basil, oregano, rosemary, salt, and pepper in a large bowl. Use your hands to form the mixture into 4 patties.
  3. Cook patties on the preheated grill until no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear, about 4 to 5 minutes per side. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
  4. Put the patties into the buns and enjoy your burger
